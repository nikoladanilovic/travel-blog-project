About me

Abdul Khabib

Even though I met a lot of people in north Africa, this guy really sticked out the most. Police officer by profession, good father, devout muslim and a frendly host, Abdul and his family have really showed me what true morrocan hospitality and tradition looks like when I visited city of Casablanca.
In Casablanca, north african city where arab and european architecture and influence meet, I've stayed for a few days at my couch surfing host Abdul Khabib. He and his wife live in two room apartment in the center of the city with their 7 year old son Mohamed. He and his wife during the Arab spring in Egypt and have moved here because of better life standard. They showed me city's beautiful landmarks, from whih Hasan II mosque has been the biggest and most memorable. Then in the evening we went to some bars and night clubs where I experienced the vibrane casablancan night life and met lots of very interesting people.


Mohamed bin al Hassan

I've met this old men in front of big mosque when I visited Kairo, the capital of Egypt, with my friends. He told me story of his life and explained some egyptian traditions that I never heard of.
Mohamed was born in Egypt as youngest in family of six. He experienced a lot of turmoil in his country like war with Israel when he was young, and Arab spring more recently. He said that Alah helped him to overcome those hard times which were even harder due to his personal problems and big family which had to be feed. Thats why now he comes almost every day to pray in this mosque to express graditude to Allah who helped him through the darkest of times.


Angelina Audibert

Native Parisian, artist and much more, Angelina has been my couch surfing host and has showed me, for the short time I was in Paris, true artistic soul and culture of that place.
Little bohemian caffes, narrow romantic streets and world class museums are nice and all, but without proper Parisian to show you Paris from up close, that is to say, from a locals point of view, all those things Paris is known for wouldn't be impressive at all. That Parisian was Angelina. She showed me lots of local caffes, and told me the story for each of them. She told me stories like, what famous singer, philosopher, etc. has regulary visited those caffes and what exiting things have happened there. She also showed me some less known museums which were even more interesting than those more famous ones.
